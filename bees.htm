Step 1: Uncap Hexagon Combs
Scrape the top of it lightly (We place these frames back in the supers and give them back to the bees). They won’t have to rebuild from scratch; they will just need to repair the caps.” 		Step 2: Full Extractor
The next step is to simply place the uncapped frames into the extractor. then turn the handle on the extractor, which spins the frames around, and the motion causes the honey to be extracted from inside the hexagons.
Step 3: Honey Drains from Extractor
The honey drains through holes in the bottom of the extractor and into a vat. On the top of the vat is a filter, which keeps wax and other stuff from going into the honey vat.		 Step 4: Strain the Honey
The honey has been draining from the vat into a food-quality bucket, but not before being strained once again through cheesecloth. Beekeepers like to call the Honey "Liquid Gold" at this stage.
