The Lavender Orpington is a new and rare color in this breed having been around for about 10 years. There is still a lot of work to be done with this color but it is improving steadily. These birds are extremely rare. The Orpington breed is named after the village of Orpington in Kent County of England. Belonging to the English class, they were bred to be an excellent layer with good meat quality. This breed was originally bred by William Cook in the late 1800's.Their large size and soft appearance together with their rich color and gentle contours make them very attractive, and as such its popularity has grown as a show bird.

They do well in colder climates. They lay about 200 medium to large light brown eggs a year. They continue laying through the winter. The hens make very good mothers. This breed have medium sized single red combs and are clean legged. Orpingtons have laid back personalities and have a timid nature about them. These are very nice looking birds that make great pets to be added to any farm.
