Animal Husbandry





The Blue Laced Red Wyandotte is one of the rarest and arguably one of the most beautiful varieties of chickens. What a spectacular example of American breeding. Our Blue Laced Red Wyandottes are sweet, gentle and elegant birds.

Blue Laced Red Wyandottes are not flighty but are energetic. They are well suited for free ranging as well as confined runs. They are sociable and vocal, uttering soft clucks regularly and are a good choice for the pet chicken owner.

Blue Laced Red Wyandottes lay an average of 220 nicely shaped light brown eggs (often speckled in white) per year and will set on occasion.

An interesting genetic characteristic of the Blue Laced Red Wyandotte is the return in the descendants of not only all blue chicks, but also the black, white, and black-white colors used in the earliest matings many generations ago to create these blue fowl. All of these chicks carry the blue genes and when mated black to white (splash) or with other blues, in turn produce numerous blue progeny. Approximately 50% of the baby chicks will be all blue. The other 50% will be black, white, and black/white combinations (splash).

Tip: Selectively breeding the black chickens to the white (splash) chickens will produce only blue chicks.
